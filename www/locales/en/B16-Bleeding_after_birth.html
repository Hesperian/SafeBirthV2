You may be able to prevent pregnancy by using emergency birth control: if you had sex without birth control or forgot to take your pills; if the condom broke; if you were raped or are suffering from sexual violence. This is called emergency contraception and it works by delaying the woman’s eggs from being released so that a pregnancy can’t even start.

Also called the “morning-after pill” or emergency pills, you can use emergency contraception during the first 5 days after you had sex—the sooner you take it, the more certain it will work. If you are already pregnant, emergency pills won't stop the pregnancy. Emergency contraception is safe for almost all women.

A good follow-up step after using emergency contraception is to choose and start a family planning method that will work for you.

  • How to use

    Special pills for emergency contraception are available in many countries. You may need to take 1 or 2 pills depending on the type—read the instructions carefully.

    If emergency contraception pills aren't available, some kinds of regular birth control pills work just as well. The mini pill, for example, has a small amount of levonorgestrel or norgestrel (both are kinds of progestin). Depending on how much is in each pill, by taking 40 or 50 pills you will get the same amount as 1 or 2 special pills. Some combined pills can also be used. See the chart below.

    How to take special Emergency Contraception pills
    Emergency pills containing 1.5 mg (1500 mcg) levonorgestrel
    (Norlevo 1.5, Plan B One-Step, Postinor-1)
    Take one pill, one time only
    Emergency pills containing 30 mg ulipristal acetate
    (ella, ella One)
    Take one pill, one time only
    Emergency pills containing 0.75 mg (750 mcg) levonorgestrel
    (Norlevo 0.75, Optinor, Postinor, Postinor-2, Plan B)
    Take 2 pills, one time only
    Emergency pills containing 50 mcg ethinyl estradiol and 250 mcg levonorgestrel
    (Tetragynon)
    Take 2 pills; 12 hours later, take 2 more pills.
    How to take combination birth control pills for Emergency Contraception
    Combined pills containing 50 mcg ethinyl estradiol and 250 mcg levonorgestrel
    (Neogynon, Nordiol) or 500 mcg norgestrel (Ovral)
    Take 2 pills; 12 hours later, take 2 more pills.
    Combined pills containing 30 mcg ethinyl estradiol and 150 mcg levonorgestrel
    (Microgynon, Nordette) or 300 mcg norgestrel (Lo-Femenal, Lo/Ovral)
    Take 4 pills; 12 hours later, take 4 more pills.
    Combined pills containing 20 mcg ethinyl estradiol and 100 mcg levonorgestrel
    (Alesse, Lutera)
    Take 5 pills; 12 hours later, take 5 more pills.
    How to take minipill birth control pills for Emergency Contraception
    Progestin-only pills (minipills) containing 75 mcg norgestrel
    (Ovrette)
    Take 40 pills one time only
    Progestin-only pills (minipills) containing 37.5 mcg levonorgestrel
    (Neogest)
    Take 40 pills one time only
    Progestin-only pills (minipills) containing 30 mcg levonorgestrel
    (Microlut, Microval)
    Take 50 pills one time only

    Progestin-only pills and special Emergency pills have fewer side effects (headaches and nausea) than combined pills when used for emergency contraception.

    Very heavy or obese women should use the Emergency pills that contain ulipristal, or consider taking a double dose of the pills containing levonorgestrel.

    If you use the Emergency pills that contain ulipristal, wait 5 days before starting hormonal methods of birth control and avoid sex or use a condom for 2 weeks to give them time to start working. It you use any of the other Emergency pills, you can start hormonal methods right away, but avoid sex or use a condom for 1 week to give them time to start working.

    If you want to be sure the emergency pills worked, wait 4 weeks and then take a pregnancy test.

  • Many women think emergency contraception is great because…
    • You can still prevent pregnancy even if your regular birth control fails or you didn't use it.
    But some women don’t like emergency contraception because:
    • It is more expensive than other methods.
    • There is a very short time in which it works.
    • The unpleasant physical signs of menstruation are often stronger—heavier bleeding, sore breasts, stronger headache, etc.
    • It can make you feel more anxious if you are not totally certain that you have taken it soon enough to prevent pregnancy.
