The mini pill contains only 1 hormone, progestin, though different brands contain different amounts. Because different amounts of progestin cause different side effects, you may find that some brands of mini pills work better for you than others.

  • How to use the mini pill

    Take your first pill on the first day of your period. Then take 1 pill at the same time every day, even if you don't have sex. When you finish a packet, start your new packet the next day, even if you haven't had any bleeding. Don't skip a day. Every pill in the packet has the same amount of progestin.

    If you take the mini pill even a few hours late, or if you forget 1 day's pill, you can become pregnant. If you miss a pill, take it as soon as you remember. Then take the next pill at the regular time, even if it means taking 2 pills in one day. Use condoms or don't have sex for 7 days. You may bleed a little if you miss your mini pill or take it late.

  • Many women think mini pills are great because…
    • When you take them at the same time every day, you are protected without having to do anything else.
    • No one can tell you are using birth control but you.
    • Your menstruation is lighter and might even stop.
    • If you want to get pregnant, you can as soon as you stop taking them.
    But some women don’t like mini pills because:
    • They can’t remember to take them at the same time every day.
    • They don’t want to use hormones.
    • They don’t want to have irregular bleeding or spotting.
    • Sometimes they can cause headaches, nausea, tender breasts, or other discomforts.
  • Who shouldn’t use it

    Don’t use progestin-only pills if you: have liver problems (jaundice, cirrhosis, tumors); ever had breast cancer; have blood clots in your lungs or legs.

    Progestin-only pills don’t work as well when they interact with certain medicines. Don’t use them if you are taking medicines for seizures, rifampicin (for TB), or ritonavir (for HIV).
