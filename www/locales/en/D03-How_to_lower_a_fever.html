A female condom is a thin plastic tube which fits into the vagina and covers the outer lips of the vulva. It can be put in the vagina any time before sex. The female condom is designed to be used only once without breaking, but if you don't have any others, it can be gently cleaned and reused up to 5 times. The female condom should not be used with a male condom at the same time. Condoms protect against both pregnancy and STIs, including HIV.

  • How to use

    The inner ring, with the closed end, goes inside the vagina, and the open end with the outer ring stays outside the vagina.

  • Many women think female condoms are great because…
    • Condoms are the only method that protects against HIV and other STIs as well as against pregnancy.
    • They are made of plastic so they can be used with partners who can’t use male condoms because they are allergic to latex.
    • They can be used with other methods.
    • Use of the female condom doesn't depend on the man.
    • Depending on your body, the external ring of the female condom might rub against your clitoris during sex in a pleasurable way.
    But some women don’t like the female condom because:
    • They can be noisy.
    • They can be awkward.
    • They can be hard to find and more costly than male condoms.
