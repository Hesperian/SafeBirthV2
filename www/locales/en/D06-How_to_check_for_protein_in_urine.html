The contraceptive sponge is a spermicide-soaked round circle of foam with a small nylon strap. It prevents pregnancy by covering the cervix so the man’s sperm can't get into the woman’s womb. Sponges only provide protection when inside you, and they can only be used once.

  • How to use

    Moisten the sponge with water. Squeeze the sides of the sponge together and insert the sponge deep into the vagina, with the strap side facing the opening of the vagina. Release the sponge and it will expand into shape to cover the cervix.

    Put the sponge in before sex, even 24 hours before if you want. To be protected from pregnancy, for at least 6 hours after sex, but leave it in no more than 30 hours. Remove by pulling gently on the strap, and then throw it away.

  • Many women think the contraceptive sponge is great…
    • It can be put in hours before having sex.
    • They don’t want to use hormones.
    • Sponges don’t affect your period.
    But some women don’t like the contraceptive sponge because:
    • You must remember to have the sponge with you.
    • It must be used correctly every time you have sex.
    • They don’t want to have to put anything into their vagina.
    • It can be difficult to insert correctly.
    • It should be used with spermicide too.
    • Chemicals in the spermicide can irritate the vagina.
  • Who shouldn’t use it

    Women who are allergic to sulfa medicines, polyurethane, or the chemicals in spermicides shouldn't use a sponge. Women with HIV should not use spermicides.
