Injections of hormones are given every 1 to 3 months, depending on the type, usually at a health center or family planning clinic. The protection lasts until you need a new injection.

Some injections are progestin-only injections, such as Noristerat (given every 2 months) and Depo-Provera (given every 3 months). Progestin-only injections may cause irregular bleeding in the middle of your monthly cycle, but this stops after a few months. Then injections may make menstruation lighter or stop completely.

Other injections contain both estrogen and progestin, such as Cyclofem and Mesigyna, and are given every month. These injections are good for women who want regular menstruation. Combined injections are more expensive than progestin-only injections.

  • How to use

    It is best to get the first injection during menstruation. This way you know you are not pregnant. You can start the injections anytime if you are breastfeeding and your menstruation hasn't returned. The injection protects against pregnancy immediately if it is given within 5 days after menstruation begins. If the injection was given 6 or more days after the beginning of menstruation, use condoms or don't have sex for the next 7 days. Follow the schedule for each particular injection:

    • Cyclofem and Mesigyna: every month
    • Noristerat: every 2 months
    • Depo-Provera: every 3 months
  • Many women think injections are great because…
    • When you get them on time, you are protected without having to do anything.
    • No one can tell you are using birth control but you.
    • With Depo-Provera and Noristerat, your menstruation is lighter, and might even stop.
    • With Cyclofem and Mesigyna, you have a regular period.
    But some women don’t like injections because:
    • They don’t want to use a hormonal method.
    • They don’t want their period to stop, or to have irregular bleeding or spotting.
    • If you want to get pregnant, with Cyclofem and Mesigyna you have to wait a month after stopping; and with Depo-Provera and Noristerat, you have to wait 3 to 9 months after stopping.
    • You might gain weight.
    • Sometimes they can cause headaches, nausea, tender breasts, or other discomforts.
  • Who shouldn’t use it

    Don’t use Depo-Provera or Noristerat if you are under 25-years old because it may keep your bones from growing as they should. Don't use injections if you: have liver problems (jaundice, cirrhosis, tumors); ever had breast cancer; have blood clots in your lungs or legs. Use a method without hormones instead.
